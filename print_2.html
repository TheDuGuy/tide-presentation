The Challenge

From "I own Tide POS" to "I use Tide POS"

  • Tide POS devices are selling well
  • Many merchants still route most volume through competitor devices
  • This is a behaviour / share-of-wallet problem, not a hardware sales problem
  • CRM's job: make Tide the first tap at the point of payment
Start with the why
  • Why they bought Tide POS: strong offer on fees / settlement, all-in-one with banking, a compelling acquisition campaign.
  • Why they still don't use it as default: staff habit with the old terminal, perceived feature gaps, reliability / speed worries, onboarding that doesn't fully deliver on the promise.
  • I'd validate these "whys" in data and merchant feedback first, then build the CRM plan around them.
Speaker Note
"Before I jump into tactics, I ask three whys: why did they buy Tide POS, why do they still reach for the competitor, and why is now the right moment to nudge them? The rest of the plan is built around validating and acting on those whys."

Strategy 2

Win the first £1,000 with Tide

£0 £250 £500 £750 £1,000
Data hypothesis: merchants who process at least £1,000 through Tide in their first 30 days are much stickier at 6 months – I'd validate this in POS data first, then design the challenge to deliberately push more merchants into that "sticky" cohort.
  • Time-bound challenge: "Process your first £1,000 on Tide in 30 days"
  • Test different rewards:
    • Reduced fees on that tranche
    • Small cashback / statement credit
    • Entry into a prize draw
  • CRM journey:
    • Kickoff email + in-app banner
    • Progress updates ("You're 65% of the way there")
    • Celebration + reward when target is hit
Speaker Note
"Most merchants have a favourite mug – their old terminal. This programme gives them a reason to pick up the new mug a few times and realise it's just as good (or better)."

Strategy 4

Find & fix the reasons they don't tap Tide

"What most often stops you using Tide POS instead of your other terminal?"
Speed / reliability concerns Missing features / integrations Staff habit / muscle memory Fees / settlement perceptions

Speed / Reliability

Education & reassurance – share performance data

Missing Features

Honest product-roadmap comms where gaps remain

Staff Habit

Training tips & quick-start guides for team

Fee Perceptions

Clear fee comparison & settlement explainers

Example journey – "Staff habit" barrier
  • Email: "Switch day" plan with three simple steps plus a printable note to put by the till.
  • Follow-up: A week later to check if the team has switched; if not, offer quick chat/support.

Aggregated survey data feeds PMM and Product – if many low-usage merchants cite the same barrier, that shapes roadmap and positioning, not just CRM journeys.

Execution

First 90 days – How I'd execute

0–30 Days
Discover & define
  • Deep dive into POS & CRM data
  • Map existing journeys & touchpoints
  • Define share-of-spend KPIs
  • Fix obvious QA issues in comms
  • Review top POS acquisition campaigns and value props; identify where sales are strong but usage is weak
30–60 Days
Launch v1 programmes
  • Launch "Make Tide default" onboarding
  • Launch "First £X" challenge for a core segment
  • Set up dashboards & basic A/B tests
60–90 Days
Scale & optimise
  • Add behavioural nudges & feedback journeys
  • Expand to more segments / verticals
  • Formalise test roadmap & present early results
  • Use over-performing segments/verticals as prototypes – replicate their value props into underperforming segments
